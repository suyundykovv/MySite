Club History

Arsenal Football Club was founded in 1886 as Dial Square by a group of workers at the Royal Arsenal in Woolwich. The club was soon renamed Royal Arsenal and became the first London club to turn professional in 1891. They joined the Football League as Woolwich Arsenal in 1893. The club struggled financially and relocated to Highbury in North London in 1913, dropping "Woolwich" from their name to become simply Arsenal.

Arsenal's first period of major success came under Herbert Chapman, who revolutionized the club in the 1930s. Chapman introduced modern tactics, brought in star players, and helped Arsenal secure their first FA Cup in 1930, and League Championships in 1931 and 1933. Chapman's innovations, such as the WM formation and white sleeves on the kit, left a lasting impact on the club.

After World War II, Arsenal won two more league titles and an FA Cup under manager Tom Whittaker. The club's next golden era came in the 1970s under Bertie Mee, who led Arsenal to their first league and FA Cup double in 1971. The team, however, struggled to maintain that success throughout the rest of the decade.

Arsène Wenger, appointed in 1996, transformed the club once again. Wenger's Arsenal played attacking football, won the league and FA Cup double in 1998 and 2002, and famously went unbeaten in the league during the 2003-04 season, earning the team the nickname "The Invincibles." In 2006, Arsenal moved to their new home, the Emirates Stadium.

Legendary Moments

Some of Arsenal's most legendary moments include:

  • 1886: Founding of the club as Dial Square, which was soon renamed Royal Arsenal.
  • 1930: Winning the FA Cup, the club's first major national trophy under Herbert Chapman.
  • 1971: Winning their first League and FA Cup double under Bertie Mee.
  • 1989: Arsenal's dramatic last-minute title victory over Liverpool at Anfield, securing the league on goal difference.
  • 2003-2004: Going unbeaten in the Premier League season under Arsène Wenger, earning the title "The Invincibles."
